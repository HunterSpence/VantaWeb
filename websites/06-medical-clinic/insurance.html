Billing & Insurance FAQ

Common questions about billing, insurance, and payments

Payment is due at the time of service. This includes:

  • Insurance co-payments
  • Deductible amounts
  • Services not covered by insurance
  • Self-pay patient balances

We'll provide an estimate of your costs before your appointment whenever possible.

Yes, we bill most insurance companies directly as a courtesy to our patients. We will:

  • Submit claims to your primary insurance
  • Follow up on claim processing
  • Handle appeals when necessary
  • Bill secondary insurance when applicable

You will receive a statement for any remaining balance after insurance processing.

If your insurance claim is denied, we will:

  • Review the denial reason
  • Contact your insurance company for clarification
  • Resubmit with additional information if needed
  • File an appeal if appropriate
  • Work with you on payment options

Our billing team will keep you informed throughout the process.

Your medical bill will show:

  • Services provided with dates
  • Total charges for each service
  • Insurance payments and adjustments
  • Your responsibility (patient balance)
  • Payment due date

If you have questions about your bill, please call our billing department at (555) 123-BILL.

Yes! We can provide cost estimates for:

  • Scheduled procedures
  • Diagnostic tests
  • Office visits
  • Dental procedures

Call our billing team 24-48 hours before your appointment. We'll verify your insurance benefits and provide an estimate of your out-of-pocket costs.

Under the No Surprises Act, we protect you from unexpected bills by:

  • Providing good faith estimates for uninsured patients
  • Following emergency care billing rules
  • Notifying you of out-of-network providers
  • Offering patient dispute resolution

You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate for the total expected cost of non-emergency healthcare services.

To apply for financial assistance:

  • Complete our financial assistance application
  • Provide proof of income (pay stubs, tax returns)
  • Submit bank statements if requested
  • Include documentation of financial hardship

Our financial counselor will review your application and determine eligibility within 10 business days.

Yes! You can pay online through:

  • Our patient portal (most convenient)
  • Our website's bill pay feature
  • Mobile app payment option
  • Phone payments: (555) 123-BILL

Online payments are secure, processed immediately, and you'll receive email confirmation. Payments can be scheduled for future dates or set up as recurring payments.
